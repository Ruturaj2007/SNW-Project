Feeding Guidelines

A balanced diet is crucial for your bird's health. Provide a mix of high-quality pellets, a limited amount of seeds, and fresh vegetables and fruits daily. Ensure fresh, clean water is always available. Avoid avocado, chocolate, and caffeine as they are toxic to birds.

Grooming and Hygiene

Regular grooming maintains your bird's physical health. Offer a shallow dish of water for bathing 2-3 times a week. Nails should be trimmed periodically to prevent overgrowth. Keep the cage clean by changing the liner daily and scrubbing the entire cage weekly with a bird-safe disinfectant.

Exercise and Mental Stimulation

Birds are intelligent creatures that require daily enrichment. Provide at least 1-2 hours of supervised out-of-cage time for exercise. Rotate a variety of safe, size-appropriate toys to prevent boredom. Social interaction with you is also key to their mental well-being.

Health Warning Signs

  • Changes in droppings (color, consistency)
  • Fluffed-up feathers or lethargy for extended periods
  • Loss of appetite or decreased water intake
  • Feather plucking or other self-mutilating behaviors
  • Any discharge from the eyes, nostrils, or mouth

Common Beginner Mistakes

  • Feeding an all-seed diet, which lacks essential nutrients.
  • Using non-stick cookware (Teflon), which can release toxic fumes.
  • Neglecting socialization, leading to behavioral issues.
  • Housing them in a cage that is too small for their species.
