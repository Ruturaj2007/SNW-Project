Feeding Guidelines

Providing a balanced diet is crucial for your dog's health. Ensure you are feeding high-quality dog food appropriate for their age, size, and activity level. Always provide fresh, clean water. Portion control is key to preventing obesity, a common problem in dogs. Follow the feeding guide on the food packaging, but adjust as needed based on your dog's body condition.

Grooming and Hygiene

Regular grooming keeps your dog's coat and skin healthy. Here are the basics:

  • Brushing: Regular brushing removes loose hair, dirt, and prevents matting. The frequency depends on your dog's coat type.
  • Bathing: Bathe your dog as needed, but not too often, as it can strip natural oils from their skin. Use a dog-specific shampoo.
  • Nail Trimming: Overly long nails can be painful and cause issues. Trim them regularly, being careful not to cut the quick.
  • Dental Care: Brush your dog's teeth several times a week with dog-friendly toothpaste to prevent dental disease.

Exercise & Mental Stimulation

A tired dog is a happy dog. Daily exercise helps manage weight, reduce behavioral problems, and strengthen your bond. Most dogs need at least 30-60 minutes of physical activity per day. Mental stimulation is just as important. Engage your dog's mind with puzzle toys, training sessions, and games like hide-and-seek to prevent boredom and anxiety.

Health Warning Signs

Be observant of your dog's behavior and physical condition. Contact your vet if you notice any of the following signs:

  • Significant changes in appetite or water consumption.
  • Lethargy, unusual tiredness, or reluctance to play.
  • Difficulty urinating, defecating, or changes in stool.
  • Vomiting or diarrhea that lasts more than 24 hours.
  • Persistent coughing, sneezing, or difficulty breathing.
  • Lumps, bumps, or sores on the skin.

Common Beginner Mistakes

Avoid these common pitfalls to build a strong, positive relationship with your new dog:

  • Inconsistent Training: Dogs thrive on routine and clear rules. Ensure everyone in the household is consistent with commands and boundaries.
  • Lack of Socialization: Proper socialization with other dogs and people during puppyhood is crucial for developing a well-behaved adult dog.
  • Ignoring Vet Visits: Regular check-ups are essential for preventative care and catching health issues early.
  • Humanizing Behavior: Attributing complex human emotions to dog behavior can lead to misunderstanding and ineffective training.
